Sampling distribution: standard error

Knowing how much a statistic varies from sample to sample (spread of the sampling distribution) is key in helping us know how accurate an estimate is. One measure of variability associated with the sample statistic is to compute the standard deviation of the sample statistics in the sampling distribution.
Note: Although this is no different from the standard deviation of sample values, the standard deviation of a sample statistic is so important that it gets its own name: the standard error of the statistic (denoted SE).
The different name helps to distinguish between the variability in the sample statistics and the variability among the values within a particular sample.
$SE=\frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}$
We think of the standard error as a “typical” distance between the sample statistics and the population parameter.
