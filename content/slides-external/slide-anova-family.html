The ANOVA family

ANOVA is not limited to the study of the effect of one factor ("One-way ANOVA"). In fact a lot of different other variations of ANOVA exist.

We will talk about two other ANOVA methods:

  • Repeated measures ANOVA: the repeated measure ANOVA allows to study and compare the mean scores of different groups in which the observational units are paired accross all the levels of the factor.
  • Two-way ANOVA: this is an extension of the One-way ANOVA that allows to examine the influence of two different categorical independent variables (two factors, each one having 2 or more levels) on one quantitative variable. The two-way ANOVA not only aims at assessing the main effect of each factor but also if there is any interaction between them.
