Comparing multiples quantitative groups (multiple means)

One-way ANOVA

  • ANOVA is short for ANalysis Of VAriance.
  • The "One" refers to the number of independant variables

ANOVA terminology:

  • the independant variables are called factors (e.g.: Noise)
  • the different categories inside a factor are called levels (e.g.: None, Low, High, etc ...).

Last time

ANOVA relies on the study of the different aspects of variability in a dataset to compare two or more quantitative groups with each other.

$F=\frac{\textrm{Mean SS}_{Group}}{\textrm{Mean SS}_{Error}}=\frac{\frac{\textrm{SS}_{Group}}{(k-1)}}{\frac{\textrm{SS}_{Error}}{(n-k)}}$

  • Between group variability, $SS_{Groups}$ : variability due to the difference in means
    (How far each mean is from the Grand mean?).
  • Within group variability, $SS_{Error}$ : variability due to the difference in values within each group. (How far each data point is from its respective group mean?)

Last time

The $F$-statistic is just like any other statistic of interest, you can apply resampling statistics to compute a p-value or confidence intervals.

Need to follow up with a post hoc test

(if you have a lot of comparisons to make, you may want to adjust the significance level for the fact that multiple comparisons are being made ... e.g.: the Bonferroni correction controls the overall error rate by setting the error rate ($\alpha$) for each test to the experimentwise error rate divided by the total number of tests)

Analyzing complex situations

Notes:

As you may have noticed, the more complex the statistic of interest we were studying (linear regression coefficients, $\chi^2$, $F\textrm{-statistic}$), the more we were relying on technology to calculate the statistic of interest for us.

If you develop some code to calculate the statistic of interest yourself, be sure to compare the result of your code to the result of functions already integrated into official packages (e.g.: Scipy stats, statsmodels, scikit-learn)

Key idea

For complex situations, it becomes more important to be able to identify which analysis to use to answer the question than knowing by heart how to calulate the statistics of interest themselves (dont' get me wrong, still, it is important to understand what those statistics of interest represent, but usually people rely on technology to do the hard work for them).
