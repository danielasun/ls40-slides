2 The p-value does not give information about the true value of the population parameter

A small p-value provides evidence against the null hypothesis (strength of evidence), it tells us that the population parameter our sample is coming from is unlikely to be the one we tested.
this does not tell us anything about the true value of the population parameter from which we obtained our sample.
