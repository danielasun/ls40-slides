The $\chi^2$ test is a general method

The $\chi^2$ is a statistic describing the discrepancy between an observed and an expected distribution pattern.

It could be calculated to study categorical variables with any number of possible outcomes (including coin flip-like experiments, with only 2 categories)

We flip a coin 20 times. We get 12 heads.
Could we doubt about the fairness of the coin?

From "fit" to "association"
