Summary: comparing multiple means

Using the $MAD$, the idea behing comparing multiple ($>2$) means is the same than when you compared only two means
How far appart are the group means from each other?

Note: While the $MAD$ is very flexible and can be adapted to characteristics of your dataset (Skewed? use the medians), a MAD analysis can only be performed using a resampling approach (its sampling distribution cannot be theoretized).

A more general approach to compare multiple means is to analyze the different aspects of the variance in a dataset (ANOVA).

The $F$-statistic compares the variability between groups (sample means) to the variability within groups (variance withing the groups). The bigger the difference between groups compared to the variability within groups, the bigger the $F$-statistic (mean $SS_{error}$ is the denominator).
