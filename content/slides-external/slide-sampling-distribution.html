Estimating the true value of the population parameter

Statistics is all about gaining knowledge about a population data by only using a a sample from this population.
Significance / Estimation
Generalization / Causation

By using significance testing, we get an idea of the compatibility of our sample with a specific population (we test a specific population parameter). This is different than getting an estimate of the true value of our population parameter.

How good of an estimate of the true population parameter is my sample statistic? How close to the true population parameter the statistic we calculated from the sample is?

Sampling from a population

We usually think of a parameter (e.g.: $\mu$) as a fixed value while the corresponding sample statistic (e.g.: $\bar{x}$) varies from sample to sample, depending on which observational units are selected to be in the sample.
We are interested in the value of the population parameter, but this usually cannot be computed directly because it is often very difficult or impossible to collect data from every member of the population.
The sample statistic might vary depending on the sample, but at least we can compute its value.

Sampling distribution

A sampling distribution is the distribution of sample statistics computed for different random samples of the same size from the same population.
A sampling distribution shows us how the sample statistic varies from sample to sample. Sampling distributions apply to every statistic that we've covered so far, and lots more!
