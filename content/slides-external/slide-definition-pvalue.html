p-value

A p-value is the probability of obtaining a result at least as extreme as the one observed, assuming that the null hypothesis is true.

A small p-value casts doubt on the null hypothesis/model used to perform the calculation (in this case, that Buzz was just guessing which button to push).

A p-value measures a sample's compatibility with a hypothesis, not the truth of the hypothesis.
