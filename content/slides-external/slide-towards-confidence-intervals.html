Estimating the population parameter

Towards confidence intervals:
Since the sampling distribution of a statistic will approximate a normal distribution centered at the population parameter, based on the characteristics of the normal distribution we can express how certain we are that a statistic calculated from a sample is close to the true parameter of the population.
We know for example that 95% of our statistics will end up withing $\pm 1.96\times SE$ distance of the parameter. This also means that for 95% of the statistics that we will observe, the true parameter will be $\pm 1.96\times SE$ away.
Such an interval is called a confidence interval.
Confidence interval are a measure of the uncertainty of the parameter we're interested in.

Confidence intervals: it's all about the sampling distribution variability

Sample Size Matters!
As the sample size increases, the variability of sample statistics tends to decrease and sample statistics tend to be closer to the true value of the population parameter (narrower sampling distribution).
The bigger the sample size, the smaller the uncertainty of your parameter, the narrower the confidence intervals.
