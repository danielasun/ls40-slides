The goal of statistics

Usually, we do not have the luxury of getting access to the full population of what we want to study.
We have to rely on a sample (from this population) to make inferences about the characteristics of the full population.

Null Hypothesis Significance Testing

Could my observed statistic ($\hat{p}$, $\bar{x}$) be coming from a population centered at a certain value of parameter ($\pi$, $\mu$)?

What would the distribution of samples statistics ($\hat{p}$, $\bar{x}$, ...) coming from this hypothesized population look like? What is the probability of observing a value of the statistic as extreme as the one I obtained from my sample in this sampling distribution ?

Resampling:
Let's simulate it and see (no assumption)

Theory:
- For a proportion, it's a normal distribution centered at $\pi$, and with a standard deviation of $\sqrt{\frac{\pi(1-\pi)}{n}}$
- For a mean, it's a $t$-distribution centered at $\mu$, and with a standard deviation of $\frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}$ (but use $\frac{s}{\sqrt{n}}$ instead).

Confidence intervals

What would be a range of values calculated from my statistic ($\hat{p}$, $\bar{x}$) I am confident will contain the true value parameter ($\pi$, $\mu$) for the population my sample is coming from?

What is the uncertainty of the parameter I estimate using the statistic from my sample?

Resampling:
Let's use our data and draw lots of Bootstrap samples. We will keep the center $95\%$ values as the range of values we are confident is most likely containing the true value of the parameter of the population our sample is coming from.

Theory:
We will consider a range of values centered at our observed statistic and of width $\pm2SE$ on each side.
$SE=\sqrt{\frac{\hat{p}(1-\hat{p})}{n}}$ for a sample proportion
$SE=\frac{s}{\sqrt{n}}$ for a sample mean

Confidence intervals (95%)

What would be a range of values I am confident will contain the true value of the population parameter my sample is coming from?

What would the distribution of samples statistics coming from this hypothesized population look like? What is the probability of my observed statistic happening in this distribution ?

What would be the variability of the central $95\%$ of the sampling distribution of my statistic if I had access to other samples from this same distribution? This interval is very likely to contain the true parameter value.
