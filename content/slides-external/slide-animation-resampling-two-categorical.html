Simulating the null

  1. Pool all the 41 wins/losses together
  2. Shuffle all 41 games and randomly redistribute into two stacks
    • One with 18 games (representing the sell out crowd games)
    • Another 23 games (representing the games without sell out crowd)
  3. Calculate the difference in $\hat{p}$ (or relative risk) for the simulation
  4. Repeat 10000 times (we obtain 10000 d or RR that we could have observed if the presence or not of a sold-out crowd did not matter)
