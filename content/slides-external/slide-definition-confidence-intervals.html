Confidence intervals

A confidence interval for a parameter is an interval computed from sample data by a method that will capture the parameter for a specified proportion of all samples. The success rate (proportion of all samples whose intervals contain the parameter) is known as the confidence level.
Note:
While confidence intervals can be constructed for any confidence level, the most common confidence intervals computed in science are:
  • 90% confidence intervals
  • 95% confidence intervals
  • 99% confidence intervals
