Null Hypothesis
Buzz is just guessing (his probability of choosing the correct button is 0.50) and he got really lucky in these 16 attempts.
$\pi=0.5$
Alternative hypothesis
Buzz is doing something other than just guessing (his probability of choosing the correct button is more than 0.50).
$\pi>0.5$

The probability of getting 15/16 correct choices just by guessing is extremely small. We have strong evidence against the null hypothesis and we can conclude that it is very likely that something other than "random chance" was at play in the experiment.
