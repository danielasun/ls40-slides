Statistical vs Practical Significance

Suppose a weight loss program recruits 10,000 people for a randomized experiment.

Because of the huge sample size, even a very small difference in average weight loss of 0.5lbs could be found to be statistically significant (lot of statistical power from the big sample size).

Suppose the experiment lasted for a year.
Is a loss of Â½ a pound practically significant?

A statistically significant result is not always practically significant, especially with large sample sizes.
